{# The signed-out shell (web-server-guide § The site). The auth service's pages are standalone public documents on their own routes (/login, /register, /reset, /forgot) — but they are not a separate *place*: they render the content service's own base.html, so the header, the panes, the footer and the stylesheet are the ones the signed-in visitor will keep seeing. That is the whole of "one layout, pre and post auth". The split: the left pane is the start page (_home.html, the same partial the content service renders — inert, because `subject` is undefined here), and the right pane holds the dialogue, standing exactly where the terminal will. A page fills `dialogue`. `error` / `notice` are rendered here for all of them, so no page repeats the flash markup.
